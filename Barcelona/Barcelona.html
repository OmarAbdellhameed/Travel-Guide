Sagrada Família

View on Map

The Sagrada Família is an iconic basilica designed by architect Antoni Gaudí in Barcelona, Spain. Construction began in 1882 and is still ongoing, with an expected completion date in the coming years. Renowned for its unique architectural style, intricate facades, and stunning interior, it’s a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must-visit for anyone exploring the city.

Camp Nou

View on Map

Camp Nou is the home stadium of FC Barcelona, one of the most famous football clubs in the world. Opened in 1957, it has a seating capacity of over 99,000, making it the largest stadium in Europe. Visitors can explore the stadium through guided tours, which include access to the museum, pitchside views, and the trophy room showcasing the club's rich history.

La Pedrera

View on Map

La Pedrera, also known as Casa Milà, is a modernist building designed by Antoni Gaudí and completed in 1912. Located in Barcelona, it features a unique undulating stone facade and a rooftop adorned with whimsical chimneys. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, La Pedrera offers exhibitions and guided tours that delve into Gaudí's innovative architectural techniques and vision.
