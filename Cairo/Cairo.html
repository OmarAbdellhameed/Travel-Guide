Pyramids of Giza

View on Map

The Pyramids of Giza, located on the outskirts of Cairo, Egypt, are one of the most iconic and enduring symbols of ancient civilization. The complex includes the Great Pyramid of Khufu, the Pyramid of Khafre, and the Pyramid of Menkaure, along with the Sphinx. Built around 4,500 years ago, these monumental structures showcase the architectural and engineering prowess of the ancient Egyptians and are UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Al Azhar Mosque

View on Map

Al-Azhar Mosque, established in 970 AD, is one of the oldest universities in the world and a prominent center of Islamic scholarship. Located in Cairo, Egypt, it is renowned for its beautiful architecture and rich history. The mosque serves as a spiritual and educational hub, attracting students and visitors from around the globe, and remains a key site for learning about Islamic culture and heritage.

Share3 Al Mo3ezz

View on Map

Sharia Al-Muizz Li-Din Allah is one of the oldest and most historically significant streets in Cairo, Egypt. It is renowned for its stunning medieval Islamic architecture, featuring a mix of mosques, palaces, and historic buildings. As a vibrant cultural and commercial hub, it offers a glimpse into Cairo's rich heritage and is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike.
