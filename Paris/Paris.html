Effiel Tower

View on Map

The Eiffel Tower is an iconic symbol of Paris and one of the most recognizable structures in the world. Standing at 1,083 feet (330 meters), it was completed in 1889 for the Exposition Universelle (World's Fair). Designed by Gustave Eiffel, the tower offers breathtaking views of the city from its observation decks. Visitors can enjoy dining at restaurants on the first and second levels, or simply take in the stunning skyline from the summit. The Eiffel Tower is beautifully illuminated at night, making it a must-see attraction for anyone visiting Paris.

Louvre Museum

View on Map

The Louvre Museum is the largest and most visited art museum in the world, located in the heart of Paris. Originally a royal palace, the Louvre houses an extensive collection of over 380,000 artworks, including masterpieces like the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo. The museum's glass pyramid entrance is a modern architectural marvel that contrasts with the historic buildings surrounding it. Visitors can explore diverse galleries, spanning ancient civilizations to modern art, making the Louvre a cultural treasure trove and a highlight of any trip to Paris.

Arc de Triomphe

View on Map

The Arc de Triomphe is a monumental arch located at the western end of the Champs-Élysées in Paris. Commissioned by Napoleon Bonaparte in 1806 to honor those who fought and died for France, the arch stands 50 meters tall and features intricate sculptures and reliefs. Visitors can climb to the top for panoramic views of the city and the famous avenues radiating from the arch. The Tomb of the Unknown Soldier lies beneath the arch, serving as a poignant tribute to those lost in war. The Arc de Triomphe is a significant landmark and an essential stop for anyone exploring Paris.
