Tokyo tower

View on Map

Tokyo Tower is a striking communications and observation tower in Minato, Tokyo, standing at 333 meters (1,093 feet). Inspired by the Eiffel Tower, it offers breathtaking views of the city from its observation decks. Completed in 1958, it remains a symbol of Japan’s post-war growth and modernization.

Mount Fuji

View on Map

Mount Fuji, Japan's highest peak at 3,776 meters (12,389 feet), is an iconic stratovolcano located on Honshu Island. Renowned for its stunning symmetrical shape, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a sacred symbol in Japanese culture. Popular for hiking during the summer months, it attracts climbers and visitors from around the world.

Shibuya scramble crossing

View on Map

Shibuya Scramble Crossing is famous for being one of the busiest pedestrian crossings globally, located in Tokyo’s vibrant Shibuya district. When the traffic lights turn red, hundreds of people cross from all directions, creating a unique urban spectacle. The area is also home to the Hachiko statue and bustling shopping and entertainment options.
